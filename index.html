Introduction

Kualiti Alam owns and operates the only integrated hazardous waste management center in Malaysia. WMC holds the license to handle 76 categories of 77 scheduled wastes listed under the Environmental Quality (Scheduled Wastes) Regulations 2005.

These facilities have the capacity to store, treat and dispose more than 100,000 tonnes of all classes of hazardous wastes annually. The end-to-end facilities comprise of:

Incineration Plant
Physical & Chemical Treatment Plant
Solidification Plant
Secured Landfill
Clinical Waste Treatment Center

Waste to Energy Process

The process transforms collected waste into usable energy through an integrated system of sorting, treatment, and recovery.

Step 1 — Waste Collection

Waste is collected and transported to the processing facility, ensuring safe handling and sorting for treatment.

Step 2 — Treatment & Conversion

Non-recyclable waste undergoes controlled incineration or physical-chemical treatment to produce usable energy.

Step 3 — Energy Recovery

Recovered heat energy is converted into electricity, powering nearby facilities while reducing landfill use.

Kualiti Alam Historical Timeline

1979–1981

Formation of National Ad-Hoc Committee on Scheduled Waste Management. Identification of potential site for disposal.

1988–1991

Government invited interested parties to build treatment & disposal facilities. Incorporation of Kualiti Alam.

1993–1996

Detailed EIA Report for Bukit Nanas approved. Phase 1 construction started. WMC began waste collection.

1997–1998

First landfill, solidification & chemical plants began operations. Official opening by Tun Dr. Mahathir Mohamad.
